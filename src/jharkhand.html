JEWELLERY OF JHARKHAND

Similar to its neighboring state Bihar, Jharkhand's jewelry tradition is characterized by tribal ornaments made from natural materials, bell-metal, and brass, with unique designs that hold cultural significance for the various tribal communities inhabiting the region.

KHONGSOO

The tribal jewelry of Jharkhand is typically handcrafted using simple tools and traditional methods. Ornaments made from natural materials like seeds, beads, wood, and animal bones are created by stringing, carving, and shaping these elements into necklaces, bracelets, earrings, and other adornments. Bell-metal and brass jewelry is produced using techniques like casting, where molten metal is poured into molds, and hammering, where the metal is shaped by repeated blows. The designs often feature geometric patterns and motifs that are specific to each tribal community and hold cultural and symbolic meaning.

Did you know? The jewelry of Jharkhand is not just decorative; it often serves practical purposes, such as protecting against evil spirits or enhancing fertility. Each piece tells a story and carries the essence of the wearer's identity and heritage.
KHANTHA MALA

These traditional jewelry forms are deeply connected to the cultural practices and rituals of Jharkhand's tribal communities. They often play a significant role in ceremonies, dances, and social gatherings, serving as markers of identity, status, and cultural heritage. The craftsmanship reflects a close relationship with the natural environment and the preservation of age-old artistic traditions.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Jharkhand jewellery lies in its deep connection to tribal heritage, craftsmanship, and identity. Traditional ornaments like Rajohar necklaces, Phoolkathi nose rings, and Kanjari bangles symbolize prosperity and social status. Crafted by indigenous communities like the Santhal, Munda, and Oraon, these intricate designs reflect their devotion to nature and customs. Worn during festivals and ceremonies, these timeless adornments preserve Jharkhand’s rich cultural legacy across generations
