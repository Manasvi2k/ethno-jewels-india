JEWELLERY OF HARYANA

Traditional Haryanvi jewelry often features heavy silver pieces and elaborate gold ornaments, particularly Kundan sets, which are prominently worn during festivals and weddings, symbolizing prosperity and cultural identity. The bold designs reflect the region's vibrant traditions.

PAJEB

The creation of heavy silver jewelry in Haryana involves skilled metalworkers who use techniques like casting, hammering, and intricate engraving to produce substantial pieces like "kada" (thick bangles) and "pajeb" (anklets). These ornaments are often designed with bold patterns and may include embellishments like ghungroos (small bells) that create a musical sound as the wearer moves. Kundan jewelry, popular for weddings and festivals, requires meticulous setting of gemstones in gold frames, often creating elaborate and opulent sets that include necklaces, earrings, and head ornaments. The craftsmanship reflects a tradition of valuing substantial and ornate jewelry.

Did you know? The "Pajeb" is not just an ornament; it is often considered a symbol of marital status in Haryana, with specific designs and styles associated with different communities. The sound of the "ghungroo" is believed to ward off evil spirits and bring good luck to the wearer.
BORLA

The significance of jewelry in Haryana is deeply rooted in social customs and traditions. Heavy silver ornaments are often seen as a symbol of wealth and status, while Kundan sets are an essential part of a bride's trousseau, signifying prosperity and auspicious beginnings. The designs often incorporate traditional motifs and reflect the region's cultural identity and artistic heritage.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Haryana jewellery lies in its deep-rooted connection to tradition, elegance, and identity. Adorned with gold and silver, ornaments like Borla, Panchmora, and Hathphool symbolize prosperity and heritage. Haryanvi brides traditionally wear Nath and Sahr, reflecting the region’s artistic craftsmanship. These timeless pieces continue to be worn during weddings and festivals, preserving Haryana’s rich cultural legacy.
