JEWELLERY OF WEST BENGAL

West Bengal is famous for its delicate gold filigree work and unique terracotta jewelry, alongside the significant Shakha and Pola (conch and coral bangles) worn by married women and the ornamental Tikli (forehead ornament), reflecting the state's artistic traditions and cultural customs.

Shaka Paula

West Bengal's jewellery beautifully reflects the region's rich culture and skilled artistry. Gold is the most common metal, crafted into detailed designs using techniques like filigree and granulation. Silver is also popular, and precious stones often add sparkle. Notably, conch shells (shakha) and red coral (pola) are culturally significant, especially for married women. Iconic pieces include the Shakha Pola bangles, a vital symbol of marriage.

Naths (nose rings) are often large and ornate. Tiklis are elegant forehead ornaments. Jhumkas (dangling earrings) feature intricate designs. The Ratanchur is a unique hand ornament. Necklaces like the Chik (choker) and Pati Haar (multi-strand necklace) are also important, showcasing exquisite craftsmanship and cultural pride.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of West Bengal jewellery lies in its deep-rooted connection to tradition, artistry, and identity. Bengali ornaments like Shaka Pola bangles, Pati Haar necklaces, and Jhumko earrings symbolize prosperity, marital status, and heritage. Crafted with intricate designs and vibrant gemstones, these timeless pieces reflect the region’s artistic excellence. Worn during weddings and festivals, they continue to preserve West Bengal’s rich cultural legacy across generations.
