JEWELLERY OF MEGHALAYA

The tribal communities of Meghalaya, including the Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia, have distinct jewelry traditions featuring ornaments made from beads, silver, and other materials, with unique patterns and designs that symbolize their respective cultural identities and social customs.

Bead Necklaces

The tribal jewelry of Meghalaya's Khasi, Garo, and Jaintia communities is crafted using materials like beads, silver, and sometimes gold, with distinct techniques and designs specific to each tribe. The Khasi people are known for their intricate necklaces made from various types of beads, often strung in multiple layers and featuring unique color combinations and patterns. The Garo tribe favors ornaments made from carnelian and other stones, often shaped into distinctive pendants and necklaces. Jaintia jewelry also incorporates beads and silver, with designs that reflect their unique cultural heritage.

Did you know?The Khasi tribe's traditional jewelry often includes a unique ornament called "Garo," which is a large, circular pendant made from silver and adorned with intricate designs. The Garo tribe, on the other hand, is known for their use of carnelian stones in their jewelry, which are believed to have protective properties. The Jaintia tribe incorporates motifs from nature into their designs, reflecting their deep connection to the environment.
Baju Bandh or Armlet

These ornaments are not merely decorative but hold significant cultural and social value within each tribe. They are often worn during festivals, ceremonies, and social gatherings, serving as markers of identity, status, and tradition. The craftsmanship reflects the unique artistic skills and cultural expressions of the different communities in Meghalaya.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Meghalaya jewellery lies in its deep connection to tradition, craftsmanship, and identity. Khasi and Jaintia women cherish gold and coral bead necklaces like Paila, worn during festive occasions. The Kynjri Ksiar pendant, crafted from 24-carat gold, symbolizes prosperity and heritage. Meghalaya’s jewellery, often adorned with intricate designs and hollow gold beads filled with lac, reflects the region’s artistic excellence.
