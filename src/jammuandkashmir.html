JEWELLERY OF JAMMU AND KASHMIR

Jammu and Kashmir’s traditional jewellery showcases intricate craftsmanship, blending Persian and Sanskrit influences with gold, silver, and semi-precious stones.

Perak

Jammu and Kashmir’s jewellery reflects its rich cultural heritage, blending Persian and Sanskrit influences with intricate craftsmanship. The region is known for its gold and silver ornaments, often adorned with semi-precious stones like emeralds, sapphires, and amethysts. One of the most iconic pieces is the Dejihor, a dangler earring worn by Kashmiri Pandit women as a symbol of marriage, believed to bring divine strength. Another striking ornament is the Jiggni, a forehead piece made of gold, often fringed with pearls and intricate engravings, worn in triangular or circular shapes.

Did you know?The region's jewellery often features motifs inspired by nature, such as flowers and leaves, symbolizing beauty and prosperity.
Nupura

The jewellery of Jammu and Kashmir is deeply rooted in tradition, with each piece carrying cultural and spiritual significance. The Gunus, a thick gold bangle featuring snake or lion head designs, symbolizes protection and strength. The Atta-hor, a pair of earrings connected by a gold chain over the head, is worn by married women, emphasizing their marital status. These ornaments are often passed down through generations, making them not just accessories but cherished heirlooms that preserve the region’s artistic legacy.

Cultural Significance

Cultural significance of jewellery in Jammu and Kashmir lies in its blend of Persian and Sanskrit influences, reflecting the region’s artistic heritage. Kashmiri women adorn themselves with Jiggni and Tikka on the forehead, Kundalas earrings, and Chaunk Phool silver crowns. Dogri jewellery, featuring Nama Dogri necklaces and Ardhchandrma crescent designs, symbolizes balance and tradition. These ornaments are treasured heirlooms, passed down through generations, preserving the region’s cultural identity.
