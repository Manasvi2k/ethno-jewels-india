JEWELLERY OF RAJASTHAN

Rajasthan is famous for its vibrant and intricate jewelry traditions, including Kundan, Meenakari, and Polki work, as well as elaborate silver pieces with tribal influences and colorful Lac bangles, reflecting the state's royal history and rich cultural heritage.

Thewa

Rajasthan’s traditional jewellery is known for its royal elegance, vibrant designs, and detailed craftsmanship, deeply rooted in its rich heritage. One of the most famous ornaments is the Borla, a spherical maang tikka worn on the forehead by Rajasthani brides, symbolizing grace and marital statue.

Did you know?Rajasthan is home to the world’s largest gemstone cutting and polishing industry, making it a hub for exquisite jewelry craftsmanship.
Bangadi

Another iconic piece is the Kundan necklace, crafted using precious stones set in gold with intricate designs, often worn during weddings and grand celebrations. Rajasthan’s jewellery beautifully reflects its royal past, with each piece representing tradition, pride, and artistry.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Rajasthan jewellery lies in its deep-rooted connection to royalty, tradition, and artistic craftsmanship. Adorned with Kundan, Meenakari, Thewa, and Polki designs, these exquisite ornaments symbolize prosperity and heritage. Rajasthani jewellery, crafted with intricate detailing and vibrant gemstones, reflects the grandeur of Rajput and Mughal influences. Worn during weddings and festivals, these timeless pieces preserve Rajasthan’s rich cultural legacy across generations.
