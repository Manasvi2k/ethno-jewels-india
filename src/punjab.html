JEWELLERY OF PUNJAB

Jewellery in Punjab is bold, elaborate, and deeply linked to wedding customs and celebrations. One of the most iconic bridal adornments is the Chooda — a set of red and white bangles worn by brides. These are often accompanied by Kalire, umbrella-shaped gold or silver danglers tied to the bangles by female relatives.

Shikarpuri Nath or Laung

Other traditional Punjabi ornaments include the Tikka (forehead ornament), Jhumkas (earrings), and Nath (large nose rings). The Raani Haar (royal necklace) and Passa (side head ornament) are popular during weddings and festive events.

Did you know?The Chooda ceremony is a significant pre-wedding ritual in Punjabi culture, where the bride's maternal uncle presents her with the Chooda, symbolizing her transition into married life.
Haath Phool

Punjabi jewellery is usually made of gold, though silver is used in rural ornaments. Mughal influence is evident in detailed work like Jadau, which is still prized for its regal appearance.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Punjab jewellery lies in its deep-rooted connection to tradition, identity, and celebration. Punjabi ornaments like Maang Tikka, Chura bangles, and Jhumkas symbolize prosperity, elegance, and heritage. Inspired by vibrant festivities and spiritual beliefs, these intricate designs reflect the region’s artistic craftsmanship. Worn during weddings and cultural events, they continue to preserve Punjab’s rich legacy across generations.
