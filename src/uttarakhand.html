JEWELLERY OF UTTARAKHAND

Traditional Pahari jewelry from Uttarakhand includes distinctive pieces like the large Tehri Nath (nose ring) and the Chandan Haar (necklace), often crafted in gold and silver with designs that reflect the region's cultural identity and the artistry of its people.

Nathuli

Uttarakhand’s jewellery reflects the region’s rich cultural identity and deep-rooted customs, especially influenced by the Garhwali and Kumaoni communities. One of the most famous ornaments is the Nath, a large, beautifully crafted nose ring usually worn by brides during weddings to symbolize prosperity and beauty.

Did you know?Uttarakhand is known for its exquisite traditional jewellery, which reflects the rich culture and heritage of the region. The intricate designs often incorporate local materials and motifs that are symbolic of the state's natural beauty and spiritual significance.
Pahunchi

Another popular piece is the Chandrahara, a multi-layered necklace shaped like a crescent moon, often made of gold and worn during festivals and family ceremonies. Uttarakhand jewellery combines elegance with traditional symbolism, making it an important part of the state’s heritage and festive attire.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Uttarakhand jewellery lies in its deep connection to tradition, identity, and craftsmanship. Ornaments like Guluband, Hansuli, and Bulaq symbolize prosperity, marital status, and cultural pride. Inspired by nature and mythology, these intricate designs reflect the artistic excellence of Garhwali and Kumaoni communities. Worn during festivals and ceremonies, these timeless pieces preserve Uttarakhand’s rich heritage across generations.
