JEWELLERY OF UTTAR PRADESH

Uttar Pradesh is known for its refined jewelry traditions, including intricate Kundan and colorful Meenakari work, as well as traditional pieces like the Guloband (choker) and Paunchi (bracelet), reflecting the state's rich cultural and artistic history.

Afgani jewellery

Uttar Pradesh’s jewellery is known for its Mughal-inspired designs and fine craftsmanship, often made using gold, silver, and precious stones. One of the most famous ornaments is the Jhumka, beautifully designed bell-shaped earrings that add grace and charm to any traditional attire.

Another popular piece is the Passa, a side hair ornament worn especially by brides, adding a royal touch to the wedding look. Uttar Pradesh’s jewellery reflects the state’s rich artistic heritage, combining elegance with timeless cultural beauty, and is often passed down through generations.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Uttar Pradesh jewellery lies in its deep-rooted connection to tradition, royalty, and artistic craftsmanship. Ornaments like Guluband necklaces, Paasa headpieces, and Shikarpuri Nath symbolize prosperity and elegance. Influenced by Mughal and Awadhi styles, these intricate designs reflect the region’s rich heritage. Worn during weddings and festivals, they continue to preserve Uttar Pradesh’s cultural legacy across generations.
