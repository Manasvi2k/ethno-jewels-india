JEWELLERY OF ODISHA

Odisha is renowned for its exquisite Tarakasi or silver filigree work, especially from the city of Cuttack. This form of jewellery involves twisting fine strands of silver into delicate lace-like patterns to create earrings, brooches, necklaces, and decorative items. It reflects centuries-old craftsmanship and is often worn during festivals and weddings.

Dokra jewelry

Beyond filigree, Odisha's tribal communities—like the Santhal, Saura, and Kondh—wear distinctive jewellery made from natural materials such as brass, beads, shells, and wood. These pieces are not just decorative but carry cultural and spiritual meanings.

Did you know?Odisha is home to the famous Jagannath Temple in Puri, where the annual Rath Yatra attracts millions. The temple's architecture and rituals influence local jewellery designs, making them a blend of spirituality and artistry.
Anklet

Traditional Odia women also wear gold jewellery during auspicious occasions. Popular items include Nath (nose rings), Chandrahara (moon-shaped necklace), and Bajuband (armlets). Jewellery often reflects motifs from Jagannath culture and regional folklore.

Cultural Significance

The cultural significance of Odisha jewellery lies in its deep connection to tradition, craftsmanship, and tribal heritage. Odisha’s jewellery, including Dokra ornaments and filigree designs, symbolizes prosperity and artistic excellence. Tribal communities like the Bonda and Kondh craft unique adornments using brass, silver, and beads, reflecting their customs and beliefs. Worn during festivals and ceremonies, these intricate pieces preserve Odisha’s rich cultural legacy across generations.
